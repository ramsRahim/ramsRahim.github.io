About

I am a PhD Student in the Thomas J. Watson College of Engineering and Applied Science at Binghamton University ( SUNY ), which is located in the New York at Binghamton. I am working as a Research Assistant ( RA ) at Real-Time Embedded Systems Laboratory ( RTlab ) which is supervised by the professor Kyoung-Don Kang.

My research interests include enhancing security within machine learning frameworks and advancing unsupervised learning techniques. I have been recognized through scholarly publications and have led impactful projects like 'Third Eye' for assistive technology and smart systems for autonomous vehicles. Proficient in various programming languages and ML libraries, my expertise was honored with a podium placement in the Ebay ML Challenge and a scholarship for technical merit.

My academic roots trace back to Bangladesh University of Engineering and Technology, where I honed my foundation in engineering, leading to advanced research in computing.

After graduating, I embarked on a journey with Cobalt Speech and Language, a USA based company , as a Research Scientist, primarily focusing on Natural Language Processing (NLP) and Understanding (NLU) tasks. I played a key role in one of the ambitious projects of my company called “Call Summarization” , training large language models like BART, BERT, and LED , thereby gaining extensive experience with transformer models.

When not immersed in algorithms, you'll find me hiking trails, playing soccer, or savoring the thrill of a good game - activities that sharpen my focus and fuel my passion for innovation.
